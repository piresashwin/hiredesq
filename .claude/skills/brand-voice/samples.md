# Brand Voice — Swipe Bank

Ready lines per persona, grouped by emotion + theme. All match the **approved north-star
voice**: calm, warm, plain, understated (see [SKILL.md](SKILL.md)). Pull, then make it true
with a real detail. India / US-IT staffing flavored. No shouting, no exclamation, no slang.

---

## The Veteran — quiet empathy → relief

**The 11pm grind**
- "It's 11pm and you're still renaming resumes. You didn't get into recruiting for this."
- "Forward the mess tonight. It'll be a clean list by morning."
- "Friday night, three tabs deep, looking for one phone number. We've all been there."

**The WhatsApp/spreadsheet CRM**
- "Your candidate list is a WhatsApp group and a spreadsheet you're a little scared to open. We get it — most desks start exactly there."
- "Five hundred chats, two hundred resumes, and your memory holding it all together. That's a lot to carry."
- "You're not disorganised. You've been running a real business out of six apps that were never meant to talk to each other."

**Quiet reassurance**
- "If a client asked what you'd billed this quarter, you'd have to guess. That's not on you — nothing ever showed you."
- "You place people. We'll handle the mess."

---

## The Straight Talker — quiet conviction → "that's just true"

**On the tools (gentle, not a rant)**
- "Recruiting's hard enough. Your tools shouldn't make it harder."
- "Your candidates shouldn't live in six different apps. One place you can search is enough."
- "You shouldn't need a week of training and an ops team to keep track of your own candidates."
- "The big tools were built for a 200-person team. You're one person doing the actual placing. Different job, different tool."

**Reframe the work (calmly)**
- "A pipeline you can't see in money terms is just a to-do list. Yours should show you what it's worth."
- "Finding the right candidate matters more than logging the activity around them."

**Free / fair**
- "The clean database is free, and it stays free. You shouldn't have to pay to see your own candidates."
- "No demo to sit through, no sales call. You forward your mess and see if it works. That's the whole thing."

---

## The Believer — quiet pride → hope

**The money, seen at last**
- "Log a placement, and for the first time you can see — not guess — what you've earned."
- "Turns out you're doing better than you thought. It just took seeing it in one place."
- "Mark the placement. There it is — what you made this month, finally where you can see it."

**You're more capable than your tools let you feel**
- "You're not behind. You just couldn't see how far you'd come."
- "One person, one desk, and a book of business bigger than your spreadsheet ever let you believe."

**Today → tomorrow**
- "Forward the chaos tonight. Wake up to a list you didn't have to type."
- "Six apps today. One clean list tomorrow morning. You'll be surprised how much was never really lost."

---

## Same idea across all three (for fast contrast)

**"You lose candidates because they're scattered."**
- Veteran: "How many good people have slipped away in a chat thread? It happens to all of us."
- Straight Talker: "A candidate buried across six apps is one you're halfway to losing — and it's quietly costing you."
- Believer: "Put everyone in one place, and you'll find most of the people you thought you'd lost were right there."

**"It only takes two minutes."**
- Veteran: "Forward it and go to bed. By morning it's clean. That's really all there is to it."
- Straight Talker: "Two minutes, no setup, no manual. A tool you need a week to learn wasn't built for you."
- Believer: "Two minutes from now you'll have the clean book you've meant to build for two years."

## Quick reference: emotion → persona → when
| You want them to feel… | Persona | Use for |
|---|---|---|
| Understood, able to exhale | **Veteran** | pain content, the chaos story, relatable Reels, replies |
| Quietly agreeing with a plain truth | **Straight Talker** | gentle POV, the anti-bloat angle, differentiation |
| A little proud, hopeful | **Believer** | revenue/wins, aspiration, retention |
