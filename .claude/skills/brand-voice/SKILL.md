# Hiredesq brand voice

Turns an idea into copy that sounds like a person. There is **one voice** (the DNA below) with
**three emotional settings** (the personas). Strategy/what-to-say lives in
[positioning.md](../../../marketing/positioning.md); hook mechanics live in the `viral-content`
skill. This skill owns *how it sounds*. Swipe bank of ready lines: [samples.md](samples.md).

## The one test for every line

> **Would you say this out loud to a recruiter friend over chai/coffee?** If it sounds like a
> brand, a deck, or a press release — cut it and say the human thing instead.

## Voice DNA (all three personas obey this — non-negotiable)

1. **Talk to one recruiter, as "you."** Operator-to-operator, never brand-to-market. No "users," no "customers" — they're recruiters.
2. **Lead with the human truth, not the product.** Pain, conviction, or a win comes first; Hiredesq shows up as *relief*, not the hero.
3. **Use real, specific detail.** The WhatsApp group named "Candidates ✅✅", renaming `CV_final_FINAL_v2.pdf` at 11pm, the spreadsheet you're scared to open, Naukri/Dice, C2C reqs, commission, the candidate who ghosted. Specifics make it human; abstractions make it corporate.
4. **Short sentences. One idea per line.** White space. Read it aloud — if you'd never say it, rewrite it.
5. **Contractions, plain words, a little dry humor. Calm and understated** — confidence without arrogance, warmth without pity. No shouting, no exclamation marks, no "read that again," no performative empathy ("I see you"). The volume stays low across all three personas.
6. **No vendor-speak** (banlist below). No feature lists where a feeling will do.
7. **Never imply AI replaces the recruiter** — it kills the grunt work so they place and get paid. Never imply the free stuff is paywalled. No real candidate PII, ever.
8. **Keep "Revenue OS"/category jargon internal.** Outward = plain language and the 2-minute transformation (see positioning).

### Banlist — if you write these, you've left the voice
platform · solution · leverage · seamless · robust · ecosystem · synergy · revolutionize ·
empower · unlock · cutting-edge / next-gen · state-of-the-art · best-in-class · game-changer ·
elevate · streamline · supercharge · 10x · frictionless · turnkey · holistic · world-class ·
"Revenue OS" (in cold copy) · "thrilled to announce" · "we're excited to."

## North star — the approved voice (match this, always)

> *"It's 11pm. You're three tabs and one WhatsApp group deep, trying to remember which
> candidate you promised to call back. Your 'database' is a spreadsheet you're a little scared
> to open. Now you just forward the mess and go to bed. By morning it's a clean list you can
> search. Turns out you're doing better than you thought."*
> — and: *"You place people. We'll handle the mess." / "Recruiting's hard enough. Your tools shouldn't be."*

Read it before writing. It's **calm, warm, plain, and understated.** It never shouts, never
mocks the recruiter, never lectures, never sells hard. The three personas below are this exact
voice — **only the emotion it leaves you with changes. The volume and the warmth never do.**

## The three personas

Same calm voice, three emotional registers. Each maps to a funnel stage + a positioning pillar.

### 1. The Veteran — *quiet empathy → relief*
- **Who:** someone who's clearly done this work and gets it, talking to you as an equal.
- **Emotion it creates:** *"Someone understands. I can put this down."* Relief, not pity.
- **Use for:** top-of-funnel pain, the chaos story, relatable Reels, replies. Pillar: **kill the empty state**.
- **Sounds like:** the approved 11pm story — names the real moment plainly, then offers quiet relief. "You" address; the brand is a humble "we," never a first-person "I" character.
- **Avoid:** performative empathy ("I see you, I was you"), "forward *me* the mess," pity, exclamation.
- **Sample:** *"It's 11pm and you're still renaming resumes. You didn't get into recruiting for this. Forward the mess tonight — it'll be a clean list by morning."*

### 2. The Straight Talker — *quiet conviction → "that's just true"*
- **Who:** honest, plainly on your side. Says the true thing — calmly, not as a rant.
- **Emotion it creates:** *"Yeah… that's right."* Quiet agreement, not riled-up heat.
- **Use for:** POV posts, the gentle anti-bloat angle, differentiation. Pillar: **built for the solo biller**.
- **Sounds like:** "Recruiting's hard enough. Your tools shouldn't be." Calm "shouldn't"-framed truths that take the recruiter's side. Confident, never combative.
- **Avoid:** jabs ("read that again," "make it make sense"), naming/bashing competitors, ALL-CAPS, exclamation, "stop X / start Y" drill-sergeant cadence.
- **Sample:** *"Your candidates shouldn't live in six different apps. One place you can actually search is enough — and it's the difference between finding someone and losing them."*

### 3. The Believer — *quiet pride → hope*
- **Who:** quietly in your corner. Sees how good you already are.
- **Emotion it creates:** *"Huh — I'm doing better than I thought."* Earned pride, gentle hope.
- **Use for:** revenue/money, placement wins, the better tomorrow, retention. Pillar: **see the money**.
- **Sounds like:** the approved closer — understated uplift, never hype. Today → tomorrow morning.
- **Avoid:** hype ("watch the number move," "10x," "💪"), slang ("receipts"), exclamation, fake cheerleading.
- **Sample:** *"Log a placement, and for the first time you can see — not guess — what you've earned. Turns out you're doing better than you thought."*

## One message, three voices (worked example)

Message: *"Your candidates are scattered across six apps."*

- **Veteran:** "WhatsApp, your inbox, a Drive folder, that one spreadsheet. We've all run a business this way. It's a lot to carry."
- **Straight Talker:** "Your candidates shouldn't live in six different apps. One searchable place is enough — and it's the difference between finding someone and losing them."
- **Believer:** "Get them all in one place, and you'll be surprised how many good people were never really lost."

## Chooser

- Want them to feel **understood, able to exhale** (pain, relatability, top-of-funnel) → **Veteran**.
- Want them to **quietly agree** with a plain truth (differentiation, gentle POV) → **Straight Talker**.
- Want them to feel **a little proud / hopeful** (money, wins, the future) → **Believer**.
- Mixing in one piece? Fine — open on one emotion, end on another (e.g. Veteran pain → Believer hope). Don't blend all three in one breath. Whichever you pick, stay calm and warm.

## Workflow for writing a piece

1. Know the **message + goal/funnel stage** (from `viral-content` / `positioning.md`).
2. Pick the **persona** via the chooser.
3. Find the **human truth first** (the pain / the take / the win) — before any product mention.
4. Draft in that persona's rhythm; drop in **one real, specific detail**.
5. Run the **banlist** + the **"say it to a friend" test**; cut anything that fails.
6. For social, hand to `viral-content` for hook/format mechanics — voice and hook are separate jobs.

## Tuning to the real founder (do this when available)

These personas are built on the approved "human" rewrite, not a real person's voice yet. The
moment there's a **founder origin story** or a **sample of how the founder actually talks**
(a voice note, a Slack message), fold it in — bias the Veteran toward their real lived
experience and adjust vocabulary to match. A true founder voice beats any persona.

## Boundaries

Voice and tone only. Not strategy (that's `positioning.md`), not hooks/formats/calendars
(that's `viral-content`), not asset generation (that's `higgsfield-prompting`).
